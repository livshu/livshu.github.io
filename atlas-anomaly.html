02 — Research Internship, LIP / ATLAS, Summer 2023

Anomaly Detection for Beyond Standard Model Physics

Context

The data produced by the ATLAS experiment at CERN is unthinkably large and complex. Approximately 1 petabyte of data per day was collected in Run 2, which is the equivalent to the data produced by Twitter every 3 months. The answers to many of the open questions in physics could lie within this data. Just one of these questions is what dark matter (DM) is. The search for DM is challenging due to the seemingly “invisible” nature of this type of matter. Interaction rarely occurs with Standard Model matter, so finding these events in the aforementioned datasets is a challenge. The LHC continues to upgrade its detectors to become increasingly sensitive to these rare DM processes. With these upgrades comes even more data than is currently produced. ML could provide an efficient solution to this data-scale problem.
