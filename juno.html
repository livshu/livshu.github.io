Overview

The Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory (JUNO) is a 20kton liquid scintillator detector located 700m underground. JUNO’s outstanding energy resolution and large volume offer the opportunity to address pressing open questions in neutrino Physics. It is widely anticipated that the neutrino Mass Hierarchy (MH) will be determined this decade, at a 3-4σ significance level after 6 years of data taking. Obtaining the spectrum of antineutrinos produced by nearby nuclear power plants to such a high precision will lead to improved determination of the neutrino oscillation parameters. The focus of this review is the determination of the MH at JUNO, acknowledging the implications this can have in other open questions such as the Majorana or Dirac nature of neutrinos and discusses how this can be done, concluding that the MH determination could benefit from other measurements including those from JUNO for a more robust measurement. JUNO has great potential for further advancing our quest in understanding the fundamental properties of neutrinos, one of the building blocks of the Universe.
