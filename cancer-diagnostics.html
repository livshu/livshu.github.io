Using Machine Learning for FTIR Cancer Diagnostics in the Clinic

The Problem

Oral cancer is on the rise in the UK, with around 12,000 new cases and 4,000 deaths per year. It's low survival rate is strongly linked to delayed diagnosis.

At present, histopathologists assess malignancy in oral lesions using optical images of biopsies. Follow-up against patient outcomes suggests predictive accuracy is only around 40%.

Using FTIR (Fourier-transform infrared) spectra from biopsies alongside machine learning, a classification method has been developed to distinguish malignant (M) from benign (B) tumours with over 90% accuracy.

One practical challenge is that the FTIR data is collected on Calcium Fluoride slides, which are more difficult to handle than the glass slides histopathologists are typically used to.
